# SolarEoL Hub
Student Research Project

## Background and Motivation
Australia is one of the world's leading adopters of rooftop solar, with over 3.5 million installations and growing. As panels from the early 2010s rollout begin to reach their 20–25 year end-of-life (EoL), a significant and largely unquantified waste stream is emerging. Solar panels contain recoverable materials, such as silicon, silver, aluminium, copper, and glass, with real economic value, yet no centralised data resource exists to help governments, recyclers, and investors understand the scale, timing, or value of this reserve. 

## Project Goal
Build a working open-source data platform — SolarEoL Hub — that quantifies Australia's end-of-life solar module reserves by state, estimates the recoverable material value, and forecasts waste volumes through 2030, 2040, and 2050 under multiple scenarios. The platform will serve as a decision-making tool for policymakers, recyclers, and researchers. 

## Why This Matters
This project sits at the intersection of sustainability, data infrastructure, and circular economy policy. The output will be genuinely useful as it is designed from the start as a public resource. A well-executed version of this platform could inform state government waste policy, support industry investment in solar recycling infrastructure, and be cited in research on the circular economy of photovoltaics.
